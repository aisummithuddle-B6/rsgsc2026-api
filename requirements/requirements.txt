## Project Title:
AI‑Driven Intelligent Claims Ingestion, Management, and Reporting for Modern RMIS
Problem Statement
Traditional RMIS platforms face critical limitations due to manual and fragmented processes:
Manual Claim Intake: Heavy reliance on form-based workflows and manual data entry.
Unstructured Inputs: Claims arrive via emails, PDFs, scanned images, and handwritten documents, requiring time‑consuming review and validation.
Slow Processing & Errors: High turnaround times, inconsistent data quality, and increased operational overhead.
Limited Real-Time Insights: Fragmented data prevents timely analytics and informed decision‑making.
No Conversational Access: Users lack a unified, natural‑language interface to query claims, track status, or generate reports.
Missed Fraud & Risk Signals: Absence of automated intelligence leads to delayed or overlooked fraud and risk indicators.
## Proposed Solution
An AI-powered, agentic RMIS platform that automates the end‑to‑end claims lifecycle:
Autonomous Ingestion: Multimodal LLMs convert unstructured inputs into structured claim records without predefined forms.
Conversational Claims Management: enable natural‑language queries, updates, workflow actions, and report generation.
Agentic Intelligence: AI agents reason across claims to detect anomalies, flag fraud, and recommend or trigger next actions.
Real-Time Insights & Reporting: On-demand dashboards and RMIS reports generated through natural‑language queries.
Tech Stack:   .NET Core for rest-API
## Business Value
Reduces manual effort and claim processing time
Improves data accuracy, consistency, and compliance
Enables faster claim resolution and better user experience
Proactively detects fraud and risk signals
Provides real-time insights and leadership visibility
Delivers a scalable, future‑ready RMIS architecture
